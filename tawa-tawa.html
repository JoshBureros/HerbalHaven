Tawa-Tawa

Tawa-Tawa (Euphorbia hirta)

Tawa-Tawa, or *Euphorbia hirta*, is a traditional medicinal herb commonly found in the Philippines. It is well known for its role in helping treat dengue fever and other ailments related to bleeding and the immune system.

Medicinal Uses:

  • Dengue Support: Tawa-Tawa is popularly used as a herbal remedy to help increase platelet count in dengue patients, although scientific evidence is still being studied.
  • Anti-inflammatory: It has anti-inflammatory properties that can help ease symptoms of asthma, bronchitis, and other respiratory infections.
  • Antibacterial and Antiviral: Extracts of the plant have shown effects against bacteria and some viruses, aiding in wound care and infections.
  • Diuretic: It helps in flushing out toxins through urination and supports kidney function.
  • Anti-diarrheal: Traditionally, it is used to treat diarrhea and intestinal worms in rural communities.
  • Antioxidant: The plant is rich in antioxidants that help protect cells from damage.

While Tawa-Tawa is widely used in traditional medicine, it should be taken with caution. Always consult a healthcare provider before using it as a treatment, especially for serious conditions like dengue.
