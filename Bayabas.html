Bayabas (Guava)

Bayabas, also known as Guava (Psidium guajava), is a widely used medicinal plant in the Philippines. It is known not only for its sweet fruit but also for the healing properties of its leaves, bark, and roots.

Medicinal Uses:

  • Wound Disinfection: Guava leaves are boiled and used to wash wounds due to their strong antiseptic and antibacterial properties.
  • Diarrhea Treatment: A decoction of guava leaves can help treat diarrhea and gastrointestinal problems.
  • Mouth and Gum Infections: Guava leaf extract can be used as a mouthwash to relieve mouth sores, gum infections, and bad breath.
  • Anti-inflammatory: The plant has compounds that reduce inflammation and are used to treat swelling and pain.
  • Antioxidant Benefits: Bayabas contains antioxidants that help fight free radicals in the body and boost the immune system.
  • Lowering Blood Sugar: Some studies suggest guava leaf tea can help manage blood sugar levels, beneficial for people with diabetes.
  • Relieves Cough and Cold: Guava leaf tea is also used as a home remedy for coughs, colds, and sore throats.
  • Skin Care: Crushed guava leaves can be applied to acne or skin rashes due to their antibacterial effect.

Bayabas is a safe and effective natural remedy commonly used in traditional Filipino medicine. While it has many benefits, it's always wise to consult a healthcare professional before using it for serious conditions or alongside other treatments.
